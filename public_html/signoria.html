Piazza della Signoria

A Vibrant Public Plaza

The Palazzo Vecchio, or old palace, continues to serve as Florence's town hall. Visitors can tour its Renaissance halls, medieval fortress and ancient ruins. Next door is the Uffizi, once the offices of Florentine magistrates and now a world-class museum with works by the great Renaissance, Mannerist and Baroque masters. By the river, jewelers' and goldsmiths' shops line Florence's Old Bridge, one of Florence's most recognizable sites. Above the shops is an enclosed corridor, designed by Giorgio Vasari in the 16th century, to allow the Medici to travel from the Uffizi to the Pitti Palace.
