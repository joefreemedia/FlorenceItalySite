Oltrarno

Across the Arno River

The highlight of the Oltrarno is the Pitti family and later owned by the Medici's, the Pitti Palace is now a complex of museums. The Palatine Gallery contains masterpieces by Raphael, Rubens, Titian, and Caravaggio. Museums devoted to silver, porcelain, costume and carriages can also be visited. Behind the Pitti Palace are the Boboli Gardens, which were laid out for Eleanora di Toledo, wife of Cosimo de'Medici, starting in the 16th century. Today it is a peaceful place to walk and view fountains, sculptures and nature. Also in the Oltrarno are the influential Renaissance cycle of frescoes by Masaccio in the Church of Santa Maria del Carmine. The scenes from the life of Saint Peter show one of the first definitive breaks toward Renaissance naturalism.
