Piazza del Duomo

The Center of Florence

In the 15th century Filippo Brunelleschi engineered what is still the largest masonry dome in the world. The dome caps the vast cathedral begun in 1296 and built atop a 7th century church. Visit the crypt beneath the cathedral and the cathedral's museum across the street. Climb to the top of the Campanile, a free-standing bell tower designed by master painter Giotto, for panoramic view of the city. Then be sure to walk down Via dei Calzaiouli. This pedestrian-only promenade is an excellent place to people watch, window shop or stop for gelato. It connects the Duomo to the Piazza della Signoria.
